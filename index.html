Our Story

Where the
Mediterranean
meets Kampala.

Olives began as a simple idea — that Kampala deserved a place where the richness of East African ingredients met the sun-drenched flavours of the Mediterranean. Two decades on, we remain that place: a garden table for curious palates, a bar for long evenings, and a kitchen that honours what grows close to home.

Every dish carries a thread back to Uganda — the Nile perch, the passion fruit, the smoky groundnut — woven alongside olive oil, preserved lemons, and hand-rolled couscous. This is not fusion for its own sake. It is cooking that knows exactly where it stands.

The Space

A garden table
in the city's heart.

Dine beneath old fig trees or inside our stone-walled dining room. Olives seats 80 guests across the garden terrace, the bar, and the private dining room.

The Bar

Evenings that
stretch long.

The Olives bar draws from Uganda's botanical abundance — local gins infused with African botanicals, freshly pressed fruit, and herbs from our kitchen garden. Our bartenders work the same philosophy as our chefs: ingredients first, ego last.

The bar opens at 3 pm and runs until the last guests leave. Weekend evenings feature live acoustic sets from Kampala's musicians.
